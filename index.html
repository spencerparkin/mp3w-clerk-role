Mueller Park 3rd Ward Clerk

Last updated: December 29th, 2024

The purpose of this page is to consicely define the role (or calling) that is the Ward Clerk for the Mueller Park 3rd Ward. It is revised as needed to keep it up-to-date, and is based upon the General Handbook and the unique needs of the ward as determined by the bishop. Absolutely no confidential information is presented here.

See also: D&C 107:99-100.

General Responsibilities

  1. Generally, the ward clerk's role is to off-load administrative tasks from the bishopric as much as possible so that they can focus primarily on the ministrative tasks of caring for the ward. Many tasks are peformed through LCR. The bishop or any of his councilers may, at any time, make a request of the ward clerk that isn't necessarily accomplished using LCR.
  2. The ability to complete some reports and audits may depend on personal knowledge of various ward members. Since I'm quite anti-social, this may require a probing of bishopric brains.

Daily Responsibilities

  1. Check LCR for pending action items (e.g., uploaded photo approval.) Take action as necessary.

Weekly Responsibilities

  1. On Sunday, attend ward council meeting, take notes, especially action items.
  2. On Sunday, discretely and inconspicously take attendance in Sacrament meeting. Record the result in LCR. (This may need to be combined with web-cam attendance as reported by the web-cam manager.) Starting the count after the Sacrament ordinance usually works well, but be sure to check the program the day-of (things can change last minute), because you do not want to start your count and then have, for example, the choir leave their seats to go up to the front in the middle of your count.
  3. Mid-week, or whenever, respond to the Ward Executive Secretary's query for items to be included in the up-coming Sunday's ward council meeting. These are follow-up items you took notes on in last Sunday's meeting.
  4. Update the state of all callings for ward members in LCR records.
    1. Update calling status of ward members in LCR, but only once sustained. Note that some calling status changes don't require sustainment, because the change is from, for example, one part of Primary to another. They are already called and set-apart in that case, so just update the info in the database.
    2. On Sunday, after all meetings, if applicable, send an inquery to the bishopric requesting info about who was set apart. Note that knowing who has been released, called or sustained is easily gleaned from meetings. (The bishopric may be too busy to respond to this inquery, so if you can find out another way, that's helpful.)

Quarterly Responsibilities

  1. Prepare and submit the quarterly report to church head-quarters when due. Organization leaders may need to be contacted to help complete the report. Note that they input data through LCR which, in turn, automatically shows up on the report.

Semi-Annual Responsibilities

  1. Perform the semi-annual membership record audit, then report the result to church head-quarters. This is done through LCR. To complete the audit, the brains of the bishopric must be used, so set aside time to meet with them in person at a computer. If they don't have all the answers, then I suppose you'd have to make plans to reach out to other people.

Occasional Responsibilities

  1. Respond directly to requests from ward members to have their records updated for accuracy. Note that proof and authorization is needed in some cases before a change can be made.
  2. Respond directly to requests from ward members to make purchases for needed supplies (e.g., baptismal rings, etc.) May need to coordinate with the finance clerk to ensure there is room in the budget.

Deligated Responsibilities

Two assistents to the ward clerk have been called to share in the responsibilities of ward clerk. As ward clerk, I have to have at least some basic understanding of what they do, because it would be my job to do it if they were ever out sick or released. And, supposedly, it's actually my responsibility, but just being deligated to them for the time being.

Assistent Ward Clerk -- Finance

This area of responsibility is the most fuzzy to me. I won't understand it better unless I'm ever tasked with doing any of it.

  1. Balance the ward budget. (Easier said than done. I have no idea how to do that.) Note that one year's surplus carries over to the next year, and this is even planned for as a way of preparing for a large expendature in a future year (e.g., girls camp.)
  2. Collect and deposit donations and tithing. This must be done with a bishopric member present. Most tithes and offerings are given electronically by church members, so there may not be much to do here.
  3. Handle reimbursements to ward members who use their own money for church-related purchases.
  4. Perform a bi-annual financial audit with the stake financial clerk. I have no idea what this involves. Clearly, though, this is to reduce errors and prevent fraud.
  5. Handle the authorized use of charitable givings toward the needs of ward members. The bishopric determines if and how such givings are used, and makes a plan with those in need so that they do not become dependent on the help being given. I co-authorize the use of the funds, print the check, and mail it if necessary. Such funds are never given to individuals. Rather, they are paid directly to third parties or for food or other types of needed supplies. Of course, such cases are to remain confidential.

Assistent Ward Clerk -- Membership Records

I'm guessing a lot here, becaue I don't know how to do this job.

  1. Record ward member ordinances in LCR for such things as baby blessings, baptisms, confirmations, and priesthood ordinations.
    1. Prepare the ordinance form and then give it to the bishop.
    2. Upon completion of the ordinance, obtain the completed form from the bishop.
    3. Use the ordinance form to record the ordinance in LCR for church record keeping.
    4. Prepare a certificate of the ordinance, signed by the bishop, and present it to the ward member for their personal records.
  2. Arrange for a ward member to give the opening prayer each week in Sacrament meeting. Likewise for the closing prayer.
  3. Some sort of record adjustment needs to happen when a ward member passes away. I am unclear on this.
  4. When a ward member moves out of the ward, send their membership records to the new ward. This can be done through LCR. Special cases exists (e.g., out of unit records are sometimes created.)
  5. Monitor LCR for membership records that newly arrive in our ward. This can be done through LCR. An automated notification, of sorts, for this exists in LCR as a tile you can setup on your dashboard.
  6. Create new records for babies born in the ward. Once born, babies become members of record, not to be confused with members of the church. Membership in the church occurs at or after the age of accountability, once baptized and confirmed.
